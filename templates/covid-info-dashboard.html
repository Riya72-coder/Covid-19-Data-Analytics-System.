What Exactly is COVID-19?

The global COVID-19 pandemic, caused by severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2), began with an outbreak in Wuhan, China, in December 2019. It spread worldwide in early 2020. The WHO declared it a public health emergency on January 30, 2020, and a pandemic on March 11, 2020. The emergency status ended in May 2023.

Declared: Mar 11, 2020 Emergency Ended: May 2023

Symptoms & Impact

How The Virus Impacts Population

Fever

The first and most common symptom is a fever, often accompanied by a flushed appearance and fatigue.

Persistent Cough

A new and continuous cough. Recommended to cough into your elbow to prevent spreading droplets.

Loss of Smell

A sudden loss of the sense of smell (anosmia) or taste (ageusia) is a unique indicator.

Project Overview

This project serves as a comprehensive tool to visualize the massive datasets released by the WHO and JHU. By converting raw CSV data into interactive heatmaps and trend lines, we aim to make the impact of the pandemic understandable for everyone.
